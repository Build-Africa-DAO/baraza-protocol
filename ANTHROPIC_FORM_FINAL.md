### Product & Usage

**Company/product description (paste as-is or trim to fit char limit)**

```
Baraza Protocol is multi-chain governance and treasury infrastructure for Africa's 13M+ chamas, SACCOs, and stokvels — informal savings groups that today run on WhatsApp spreadsheets and verbal voting. Baraza replaces that with Stellar treasury settlement (M-Pesa → XLM in under 60 seconds, 1% fees), Solana governance voting (5 Anchor programs), and Akili, an AI governance co-pilot built on Claude.
```

**How are you currently using or planning to use Claude? (paste as-is or trim to fit char limit)**

```
Claude Sonnet 4.6 powers two things: (1) Akili's real-time community chat brain (/api/agent/chat, SSE streaming) that answers governance questions and drafts proposals; (2) the Akili Council, five specialized one-shot agents — Amara (community sentiment), Kofi (governance/proposal drafting against group bylaws), Zara (regulatory compliance — Kenya CMA, Tanzania BRLA), Nia (market/yield research), Seku (educational content). Claude never makes binding decisions — it surfaces options and flags risk; final authority stays with group members via on-chain vote.
```

**What is your current usage level and projected spend? (paste as-is or trim to fit char limit)**

```
Testnet today (8 pilot groups, 100+ daily active users). Mainnet launch targeted Q3 2026. At our Year 1 target of 500 groups / 50,000 users, projected Claude spend is ~$10–15K/month across the chat brain and 5-agent council.
```

**Anything else you'd like us to know?**

```
Building on Stellar (primary, settlement) and Solana (governance) for an underserved market — 73% of the working-age population in our core markets (Kenya, Tanzania) lacks access to formal financial services, yet these informal groups collectively manage an estimated $50B+ annually with zero transparent infrastructure. SDF's $5,000 investment validates the Stellar rails approach; Claude's reasoning depth enables the governance intelligence layer.
```
